Timeline

1911

On June 24 of this year, Juan Manuel Fangio was born in the city of Balcarce. In reality he was born on the 23rd, but in the Civil Registry he is listed as born on the 24th.

1940

Between September 27 and October 12, he wins the XXV Gran Premio Internacional del Norte, 13 stages, driving a Turismo Carretera Chevrolet. He is consecrated as champion of Turismo Carretera.

1948

In a Chevrolet, he races the Grand Prix of South America, known as the Buenos Aires - Caracas race. In Peru, he overturns and his passenger loses his life.

1950

In his first season in Formula 1 he finished second in the championship. That year Fangio won the Grand Prix of Monaco, Belgium and France. Throughout the season he drove an Alfa Romeo 158.

1951

Fangio wins the world championship for the first time on his Alfa Romeo 159. On this occasion, Fangio wins the Swiss, French and Spanish Grand Prix.

1952

Fangio signed for Maserati, and hoped to retain the crown. A terrible accident in a race at Monza before the season produced a fracture in his neck, which sidelined him from the track for the whole season while he recovered.

1953

Fangio returns to Formula 1 with a Maserati A6GCM-53, but it was not until the Dutch Grand Prix, when the Maserati A6SSG was introduced, that Fangio began to make progress. Fangio wins the last race of the season in Monza, Italy. This secured him second place in the championship.

1954

Fangio signs with Mercedes, but the car was not presented in the first two races, and he raced with Maserati, winning both. By the third race, in France, the Mercedes was ready. Fangio started from pole position and won. He won the Grand Prix of Argentina, Belgium, France, Germany, Switzerland and Italy, winning his second F1 title.

1955

Fangio with Mercedes W196 R wins his third championship with four victories, one second place and one retirement. The victories were in Argentina, Belgium, Holland and Italy. He retired in Monaco and finished second in England. The season was overshadowed by the death of his friend and most difficult rival, according to Fangio's words, Alberto Ascari.

1956

Fangio joins the new Lancia-Ferrari team and drives the Ferrari D50. With victories in Argentina, England and Germany, he wins his third consecutive title, fourth to his name.

1957

Fangio returns to Maserati and wins his fourth and last consecutive title. He drives the Maserati 250F and wins in Argentina, Monaco, France and Germany, in what was perhaps the best race of all time in Formula 1.

1958

It was the last year Fangio raced, only competing in Argentina and France, where he finished fourth on both occasions. In his last race, in France, his Maserati was struggling to finish when the race leader, Mike Hawthorn, got behind him. Hawthorn slowed down and allowed Fangio to cross the line in front of him and not take him a lap down as the race ended, and is perhaps a clear sign of the high respect the other racers had for Fangio. All his contemporaries recognized him as 'the master'.

1969

Between August 19 and 22, Juan Manuel Fangio heads the Argentine delegation, which competes in the 84 hours of the Nurburgring with three Torino 380W.

1995

In the early morning of June 17, he died in the city of Buenos Aires at the age of 84.
